Unsupervised Domain Adaptation

Posted on Wed, Oct 5, 2022 Paper Notes Domain Adaptation Unsupervised Learning

1 Abundant labeled source & abundant unlabeled target

Both the source domain and target domain have abundant samples.

1.1 Domain-invariant feature learning

Divergence

Minimize the divergence between the distributions of the source and target domain.

Gretton, Arthur, et al. "A kernel two-sample test." The Journal of Machine Learning Research 13.1 (2012): 723-773.

Reconstruction

Reconstruct the data from a domain-invariant feature and a domain-specific feature.

Bousmalis, Konstantinos, et al. "Domain separation networks." Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems 29 (2016).

Adversarial

Adversarially optimize the feature extractor and the domain classifier.

Ganin, Yaroslav, et al. "Domain-adversarial training of neural networks." The Journal of Machine Learning Research 17.1 (2016): 2096-2030.

1.2 Domain mapping

Most of these approaches perform pixel-level mapping, i.e., image-to-image translation. An alternative approach is to perform feature-level mapping.

Hong, Weixiang, et al. "Conditional generative adversarial network for structured domain adaptation." Proceedings of the IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition. 2018.

1.3 Normalization statistics

Normalization layers such as batch norm.

1.4 Ensemble methods

Voting or averaging the outputs of the models to guide learning or using the ensemble to measure prediction confidence for pseudo-labeling target data.

1.5 Target discriminative methods

Move decision boundaries into regions of lower density.

2 Abundant labeled source & scarce unlabeled target

The source domain has abundant samples but the target domain has scarce samples.

  • Relax the reconstruction-based cycle consistency by task-specific loss (e.g. preserving identity for face recognition tasks).
Hosseini-Asl, Ehsan, et al. "Augmented cyclic adversarial learning for low resource domain adaptation." arXiv preprint arXiv:1807.00374 (2018).

3 Scarce labeled source & abundant unlabeled target

3.1 Scarce labeled source + abundant unlabeled source

  • Both in-domain and cross-domain contrastive learning between each sample and the cluster center; minimize the entropy for each unlabeled sample.
Yue, Xiangyu, et al. "Prototypical cross-domain self-supervised learning for few-shot unsupervised domain adaptation." Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition. 2021.

3.2 Scarce labeled source only

  • Leverage local descriptors for the few-shot setting.
Huang, Shengqi, et al. "Few-shot Unsupervised Domain Adaptation with Image-to-Class Sparse Similarity Encoding." Proceedings of the 29th ACM International Conference on Multimedia. 2021.
  • Leverage meta-learning for the few-shot setting.
Yang, Wanqi, et al. "Few-Shot Unsupervised Domain Adaptation via Meta Learning." 2022 IEEE International Conference on Multimedia and Expo (ICME). IEEE, 2022.
